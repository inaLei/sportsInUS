Icehockey

The hockey sport has it origins in the 1840s till the 1875s. The Ice rink is circa 30 meters wide and 60 meters long. Per team there are 5 players on the ice and one goaltender. A hard rubber disc, the puck, needs to go inside the other teams goal.

Face-off

After every game interruption there is a new Face-off. For that, two players, one from each team, face each other. They meet at a bullypoint, where the referee drops the puck on the ice. This process can repeat more than 50 times per game.

General about the game

In a game there are three thirds with 20 minutes each. In total there are 60 minutes. During the game a clock runs along, which stops at every interruption of the game. The break inbetween the thirds are, usually, 15 minutes long. If the game is undecided after the regular playing it comes to an overtime.

The winner of the game can also be chosen by a shootout. A goal is declared valid when the puck has completely crossed the goal line. In ice hockey, unlike in other sports, if a video recording is made, it is permitted to use to make a decision.

Offside

Offside is when both skates of a player are completely in the attack zone, that means behind the blue line, before the puck crossed that line completely. If the game gets interrupted by rule violation the referee search for a neutral zone, for the bully.

Unauthererized long shot

Icing is a free shot, which is taken from the own half behind the opponent's goal line. The referee blows the whistle when the puck has completely crossed the goal line. But if a player of his own team touches the puck before it crossed the red line, the icing shall be considerd cancelled.

Change

The players can be changed, while the entire game. It is a sport that is very fast and requires a lot of strengt. That's why a player is nearly never longer than two minutes, without a pause on the ice. The teams are usually changing in rows through out the game. For that the coaches put three to four rows, consisting of two or three players, together.

Penalties

In case of a foul, the player is sent off the field for a certain time. This player may not be replaced by another player during this time. As a result, this team plays shorthanded. For lighter offenses the penalty time is two minutes. For more serious fouls a penalty of fives minutes of the game will be imposed. Ten minutes of penalty time are povided, when there are personal fouls or also in case of unsportsmanlike conduct. If a foul ends with an injury to an opponent, a disciplinary penalty is assessed for the remainder of the game. The most common penalty is for a hight stick. Obstruction by holding on and to trip somebody up are other reasons for a penalty. Ice hockey is a very physical sport. Nevertheless, illegal harshness can also be penalized. A goaltender may also receive a penalty, but then he does not have to leave the ice. The team must select another player to serve the penalty for him.

Goal area

The goaltender enjoys an exceptionally high level of protection in this game. The so-called goal area offside shows the strict rules for this. If the goaltender is in the goal area, no player of the opposing team may come too close to him.

Empty Net

An empty net happens most of the time, when one of the teams is only one goal (sometimes two goals) behind. Then they take their goaltender out of the net and put rather another forward or defenseman on the ice. Now the other team has to play shorthanded, like that it is easier for the trailing team to score the equalizer. If they actually score that goal, they have a chance to win again. The game does not have to be interrupted by a referee, when the trailing team takes out their goaltender. An empty net can also happen, when a penalty is displayed. Then the team who the penalty is not against takes out their goaltender so the other team has to play shorthanded even before the actual penalty is given. This is without any risks, because if one of the opposing players touches the puck, the referees stops the game.
